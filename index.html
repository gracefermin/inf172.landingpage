About Your Host

Learn from a leading expert in behavioral psychology and digital behavior

Sean Young, PhD, MS

Executive Director of the University of California Institute for Prediction Technology, the Center for Digital Behavior, and Medical School Professor at UCI.

Dr. Young specializes in digital behavior and prediction technology—understanding how and why people use social media, mobile apps, and wearable devices. His work helps predict and change future behaviors in health, medicine, politics, and business.

As an internationally recognized speaker, he has presented at the European Parliament, World Congress, and various corporations and academic institutions. He is a board member within the National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine's Division of Health and Medicine.

Episode 1

The AI Conversation Your Child Needs (And How to Start It Tonight)

Most parents wait until their child encounters AI in school—but by then, habits are already forming. In this episode, Dr. Sean Young shares the exact framework to introduce AI concepts at the dinner table, addressing common fears while building curiosity. You'll learn the three questions every parent should ask, how to discuss AI "mistakes" productively, and why comparing AI to calculators might be setting kids up for unrealistic expectations.

Episode 2

When ChatGPT Does Your Kid's Homework: Setting Boundaries That Actually Work

Teachers are finding AI-written essays, and parents are caught in the middle—is using AI cheating, or is it the future of learning? This episode cuts through the confusion with practical guidelines you can implement today. We'll explore real scenarios from K-12 classrooms, share research on AI's impact on learning retention, and give you language to help your child understand the difference between AI as a tool versus a crutch.

Episode 3

Protecting Your Child's Privacy in the Age of AI

Every time your child uses an AI tool, they're sharing data—but what happens to it? This episode addresses the privacy concerns keeping parents up at night. Learn which AI tools are safe for kids, what data is actually being collected, and how to teach your child to protect their personal information. We'll cover COPPA regulations, school district policies, and give you a simple privacy checklist you can use before allowing any AI tool in your home.
